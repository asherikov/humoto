1 This is Humoto - a software framework for manipulation of linear least-squares
2 problems with (in)equality constraints. It supports both weighting and
3 lexicographic prioritization and can be characterized as a tool for goal
4 programming. However, the development was driven by works in other fields -
5 robotics, control, and numerical optimization; for this reason our terminology
6 and interpretations are different.
7 
8 
9 The core functionalities of Humoto are formulation of least-squares problems
10 and their resolution with the help of various third-party software using
11 uniform programming interfaces. However, due to our interest in robotic
12 applications, the framework also facilitates formulation and implementation of
13 optimization problems for control of robots, in particular, model predictive
14 control problems. For the same reason, we pay special attention to
15 computational performance in order to be able to employ the framework in
16 real-time scenarios.
17 
18 
19 In addition to the core components, the distribution of Humoto includes several
20 modules - implementations of specific controllers. The modules serve as
21 examples of using the framework, but can also be used in accordance with their
22 primary purpose.

46 * [PDF manual](https://bip-team.github.io/humoto/humoto_doc.pdf) overviews the
47  main concepts and contains mathematical derivations. If the precompiled
48  version is not available, you have to go to '`doc/latex`' and execute
49  '`make`' to compile it.

119 What is the difference between humoto and ... {#md_readme_faq_comparison}
120 ---------------------------------------------
121 
122 * [**RobOptim**](http://roboptim.net/)?
123  Humoto and RobOptim were developed with one similar goal in mind:
124  interfacing various optimization solvers in robot control applications.
125  There are, however, many differences. Humoto cannot deal with general
126  nonlinear problems as RobOptim does, but, unlike RobOptim, Humoto supports
127  hierarchical (lexicographic) optimization problems. Moreover, our framework
128  standardizes and automates formulation of optimization problems using the
129  concept of '[task](@ref md_howto_tasks)'. Finally, we pay more attention to
130  performance, e.g., Humoto supports more hot-starting options and provides
131  [functionality](@ref etools) for blockwise matrix operations with sparsity
132  support.
133 
134 
135 * [**SOTH**](https://github.com/stack-of-tasks/soth) (SOT Hierarchical solver )?
136  Humoto is a framework which is designed to provide common interface to
137  various solvers, including hierarchical (lexicographic) solvers, while
138  SOTH is a particular hierarchical solver.
